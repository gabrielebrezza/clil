Early Discoveries

Hydrogen was first identified as a distinct substance in the 16th century by Paracelsus. Its flammability was noted by Henry Cavendish in the 18th century.

Industrial Applications

Throughout the 19th and 20th centuries, hydrogen found applications in various industries, including the production of ammonia for fertilizers and as a fuel in rockets.

Modern Developments

Today, hydrogen is being explored as a clean energy source, with research focusing on improving production methods and expanding its use in transportation and industry.
