Hydrogen Fuel Cells

Hydrogen fuel cells generate electricity through a chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen, emitting only water vapor as a byproduct. This technology is used in various transportation modes, including buses, trucks, and trains.

Advantages

  • Quick refueling times: Hydrogen vehicles can be refueled in minutes, unlike battery electric vehicles that may take longer to charge.
  • Longer range: Hydrogen vehicles typically offer a greater driving range compared to their battery counterparts.
  • Lower weight: Hydrogen fuel cells are lighter than traditional batteries, making vehicles more efficient.
